The B-52 Stratofortress (BUFF) is the oldest plane still in service with the US Air Force beating only the C-130 by one year. The B-52 was used extensively during the Cold War there were a number of B-52 were constantly in the air so if the Soviet Union were to launch a nuclear attack the US would send these B-52 to the Soviet Union immediately as a retaliatory effort. This caused the B-52 to be under much stress and caused it to have a lot of maintenance issues such as some B-52s falling apart mid-air and consequently causing nuclear bombs being dropped onto US soil. The B-52's current mission in the Air Force is to use its bomb bay as a launch bay for multiple cruise missiles instead of doing conventional bombing missions. It has a 185 ft wingspan and is 159ft in length maximum payload weight is 70,000 lb of mixed ordnance, bombs, mines, and missiles. Its range is 8,800 miles with a service ceiling of 50,000ft.

The F-22 Raptor is an air dominance fighter developed from the Advanced Tactical Fighter program, it's competitor in the program was the YF-23 Black Widow II. It was introduced in 2005 making it the third stealth plane within the US Air Force the others being the F-117 the first stealth plane and the B-2 the current stealth bomber. It has a wingspan of 44ft and a length of 62ft. The F-22 engines have the ability to thrust vector which let the F-22 be more maneuverable than the average fighter jet. The range of the F-22 has a range of 1,850 miles with external fuel tanks it's max speed mach 2 (1,534) with supercruise its max service ceiling is above 50,000 ft. Its armament consists of one M61A2 20mm cannon, internal side weapon bays that carry two AIM-9 IR missiles and another internal weapon bay that can carry six AIM-120 active radar-guided missiles in the internal bay can also carry two 1,000lb GBU-32 JDAM and two AIM-120s instead of six AIM-120s. It has a unit cost of $143m per plane.

The C-17A Globemaster III is the second largest cargo plane within the US Air Force fleet with the biggest being the C-5 Galaxy. The C-17 has the unique ability to take off from shorter than normal runways for a plane of its size it also can land on runways made of dirt instead of just runways made of asphalt. It has a wingspan of 169 ft and a length of 174 ft it's cargo compartment is 88ft in length, a width of 18 ft, and a height of 12 ft. It has a service ceiling of 45,000 ft, and its range is global with in-flight refueling or 7,169 miles without in-flight refueling. It can carry 102 troops or 170,900lb of cargo with it's 18 pallet positions.
